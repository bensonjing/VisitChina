TIBET

The Tibet Autonomous Region is located in the southwest part of the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau. The region covers more than 1.2 million square kilometers, accounting for one eighth of China’s total land mass, and ranking second in China. Tibet has various complex landforms such as high and steep mountains, deep valleys, glaciers, bare rocks and gobi deserts. All places in the region lie at an average altitude of more than 4,000 meters. It is bitterly cold in winter, with a marked difference in temperature between daytime and night.
